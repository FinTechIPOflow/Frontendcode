Frequently Asked Questions?

Find answers to common questions that come to your mind related to IPO.

How to Subscribe to an IPO?

  • Step 1: Login to your respective service provider.
  • Step 2: Click on the IPO button.
  • Step 3: Select the IPO you want to bid and enter the relevant details.
  • Step4: Your subscription will be completed once you make the payment or give permission.

Should I buy an IPO first day?

It depends on the company's fundamentals, valuation, and market conditions. Research thoroughly before investing on the first day as IPOs can be volatile.

How do you know if an IPO is good?

Evaluate the company's financial health, growth prospects, management team, competitive position, and IPO pricing. Also consider market conditions and the company's industry outlook.

How to check IPO start date?

You can check IPO start dates on stock exchange websites, financial news portals, brokerage platforms, or IPO tracking websites like BlueStock.

What is issue size?

Issue size refers to the total amount of money a company aims to raise through its IPO by issuing a specific number of shares at the set price range.

How many shares in a lot?

The number of shares in a lot varies for each IPO. Companies determine the lot size based on their share price to make it accessible to retail investors.

How is the lot size calculated?

Lot size is typically calculated to ensure the minimum application amount falls within a specific range (often ₹10,000-15,000). It's determined by dividing this target amount by the IPO price.

Who decides the IPO price band?

The company and its investment bankers/book running lead managers (BRLMs) decide the IPO price band based on company valuation, market conditions, and investor feedback from roadshows.

What is IPO GMP?

IPO GMP (Grey Market Premium) is the premium amount at which IPO shares are traded in the unofficial/grey market before they are listed on the stock exchange. It indicates market sentiment about the IPO.

How many lots should I apply for IPO?

This depends on your investment capacity and strategy. Retail investors can apply for a minimum of one lot and a maximum of 13-14 lots (depending on the IPO). Consider your risk tolerance and portfolio diversification.
